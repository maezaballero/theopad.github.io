Epilogue

Ijbol67 67K views

Time flew by.

It is now graduation.

Mae's graduation. My time has not come yet.

I watched from the back as Mae walked up to the podium.

Mae adjusted the microphone, looked out towards the crowd, then spoke.

· · ·

There is something we don't talk about enough in school.

We talk about grades. We talk about leadership. We talk about achievement and excellence. We talk about what it means to be the best version of yourself. These things matter. But there is something underneath it all that we rarely mention out loud.

The weight of it all.

I carried mine for a long time without telling anyone. I told myself it was strength. I told myself not to make a crack in something that had to stay whole. I told myself that the right thing to do was to hold it together, smile in the hallway, and keep going.

But someone taught me otherwise.

Someone showed me that being truly seen, not for what you perform but for what you carry, is not a weakness. It is the beginning of something honest. That honesty, offered gently and unconditionally, is one of the best things one person can give another.

So if there is anything I want to leave with you today, it is to notice the people around you. Stay with them. Ask how they are doing, and mean it. You may never know how much it matters.

It mattered more than I can say.

· · ·

When Mae stepped down from the podium, I smiled.

Then the host's voice echoed across the room.

Miguel Antonio E. Zaballero
Batch Valedictorian

I cried.

That was enough.
