Chapter One

First Encounter

MZ 6.7K views

The first time I met Mae was not in the classroom nor some big school event. It was in the school lobby.

I finished my lunch early today. Great! I decided to walk around school to pass the time. It was honestly relaxing to watch how everyone else spent their break. The lower batches were running around. The upper years were crowded around their usual tables. My friends claimed the benches near the lobby like they always did.

“Christine, come here! Look at this,” Bea called out, waving her hand to signal me to go there.

I walked over. My friends were talking about the music video of Blackpink's soundtrack for their new video game.

"Okay but Jennie is still the best," Bea said, dead serious.

"You always say that," Lara replied. "It's literally always Jennie with you."

"Because I'm always right."

I laughed. We went back and forth like that for a while. It was the usual nonsense, the kind of conversation that didn't need to go anywhere. We didn't need a reason to talk. We just did.

While we were happily chatting, I noticed someone near the entrance of the lobby. They were just standing there, a little off to the side, not really going anywhere. I didn't think much of it at first.

Until Bea nudged me.

"Hey, see that person over there?"

I glanced over. "Yep?"

“Don't know... but isn't something a bit odd? They've just been standing there since you came here."

I looked again. She wasn't wrong. Everyone else around us was either talking to someone or walking around. But this person was just… still. As if they were waiting for something. Or thinking about something they couldn't quite figure out.

"Maybe they're just resting," I said.

"Really? Standing still... for ten minutes?"

I didn't have an answer for that.

"Someone should check on them," Lara said, already looking at me.

"Why are you looking at me?"

"Because you're the least awkward one here."

I sighed. "Fine."

So I ended up being the one to walk over. It would be quick “hi, are you ok, cool, bye.” Nothing more than that.

"Hello," I said.

They looked up. Up close, I could see it was someone from the upper years. They didn't look distressed or anything. If anything, they looked almost… calm. The kind of calm that felt practiced.

"Hi," they replied.

"So… what are you thinking about?"

Then came silence. After a while, they replied, "Nothing important. Just thinking."

"About what?"

They looked around slightly, like they were deciding whether or not to answer. "Everything, I guess..."

I nodded, even though I didn't fully understand. There was something about the way they said it that made it feel more honest than most things people said out loud. At the same time it felt like they weren't fully honest, or at the very least, open with sharing what's really on their mind.

I then realized we didn't really introduce each other yet.

"I'm Christine," I said.

"I'm Mae."

Suddenly, the bell rang.

The sound that told everyone to return back to their classrooms. Mae glanced up at the ceiling for a second, as if already expecting it.

"See you around," I said.

Mae gave a small smile.

"See you," Mae said, before turning and heading up the stairs, disappearing into the crowd of upper years returning back to their rooms above.

I walked back to my friends.

"So?" Bea asked immediately. "Who's that?"

"Mae. Just thinking."

Lara raised an eyebrow. "Just thinking?"

"Yep. That's what Mae said."

"Thinking? Like that? For ten minutes? So mysterious, so nonchalant."

"It's really not, Lara."

"It kind of is though." Bea looked towards the staircase as if she could still see Mae. "Mae didn't eat lunch with anyone. Just… alone."

I looked back too, even though Mae was already long gone.

"You know, it's ok to like being alone sometimes," I said.

But even as I said it, something appeared at the back of my mind. Not anything big.

Not anything I could name yet.

And that was my first meeting with Mae.
