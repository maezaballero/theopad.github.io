Chapter Seven

Valentine's Day

Ijbol67 67K views

It is now February.

As always, it seems as if the student council has yet another project for the occasion. This time, they set up a small booth at the lobby where students could send anonymous letter to their special someones for a small delivery fee.

I arrived at school not really thinking about it. Valentine's Day was never a particularly significant occasion for me. Sad.

But then I saw Mae in the hallway.

That alone was not unusual. I have been seeing Mae in the hallway for a few months now. What was different was that Mae was laughing. Not the controlled kind of laughing that I noticed before. Mae was laughing openly, at some joke one of my friends said.

Mae with my friends. Things really have changed indeed.

I stopped for just a second.

It was a small thing, but I have been watching long enough to know how significant small things could be for Mae.

· · ·

We had a study session on the day before Valentine's Day. Mae arrived a few minutes after me this time, which was like the first ever time this happened.

"You look different," I said.

"Different? How?"

"I don't know. Less like you're holding something."

Mae considered that for a moment. "Maybe I am. A little."

That was all either of us said about it. We opened our notes and got to work.

At some point during the session, Mae made a small mistake while solving and said, almost casually, "Oops. That was wrong. Let me redo that."

No self-directed frustration. No tightening.

I didn't point it out. I just remembered it quietly, the way I always remembered things.

Progress was never loud. But it was still progress.

· · ·

Valentine's Day itself was a Wednesday.

Surprisingly, the letter delivery project was apparently successful because by the time Valentine's arrived, the student council members were busy going everywhere to deliver the letters. Some of them even did dramatic acting as well.

I received two letters from Bea and Lara, both mainly containing some corny jokes.

I was in our classroom when someone placed an envelope on my desk.

I looked up.

Mae was already walking away.

"Wait...”

"Read it later," Mae said, without turning around, and then disappeared.

I looked at the envelope. My name was written on the front in Mae's handwriting. It was the same handwriting I saw on the side of some notes once, with words like unacceptable and no second chances. This time it was just my name.

I turned it over and opened it carefully.

Inside was a single folded piece of paper. I opened it.

· · ·

Christine,

I'm not very good at saying things out loud. I think you already know that about me. So I wrote this instead, because writing feels a little more comfortable for me, and because you deserve to actually hear it properly.

Thank you for noticing not my grades nor the things I perform. Thank you for noticing the other parts. The parts I didn't think anyone could see. I spent so much time making sure no one would, and then you just did, without me doing anything at all.

Thank you for not running away with it. For not making it into something I had to manage or explain. You just stayed, and I didn't know how much I needed that until you were already there.

I'm still figuring things out. I don't think I'll have it all sorted before I graduate, but for the first time in a while, it doesn't feel like something I have to figure out completely alone.

Here are some lyrics from a song that's stuck in my head lately.


There are certain days
Days when I suddenly feel all alone
When I feel like I don't belong anywhere
And my head is hanging down low

I just wanted to hide
Didn't wanna face the world
As if everything has lost meaning
As if I have lost meaning
I just sat there still

Then you appeared before me
With a warm smile, you held out your hand

You make me feel special
No matter how the world brings me down
I smile again because you're there
That's what you do

One moment I feel like I'm nothing at all
Like no one would notice if I were gone
But then when I hear you calling me
I feel loved, I feel so special

I think that's you. That's what you did.

Happy Valentine's Day.

—Mae

· · ·

I read the letter. Twice. Mainly because of that song. Nice move.

Then I folded it carefully and put it back in the envelope.

I thought about Mae writing this. Finding the words. Choosing to hand it over before I could react. Mae was still learning and working out how much of the door to open and how quickly.

Opening it, though, that was the thing.

· · ·

Going home that day, I thought about everything that happened in the past months.

A person standing alone in a lobby while the world moved around them. Notes written in red ink that no one was supposed to see. Conversations on the balcony at the end of a long night. A busy Christmas Party standing in contrast with the quiet world outside it.

I started this looking for something to uncover. A hidden truth behind the perfect image. In the end I found it, but it wasn't what I expected. It was just a person, carrying more weight than anyone around them knew, in an environment that never taught them that putting something down was allowed.

That was the thing about the world we were in. Grades and performances are all rewarded, but the exhaustion behind them is not discussed. The fear underneath excellence was also left out. People like Mae learned to keep all that with themselves, to write unacceptable to themselves and to stand alone at Christmas parties.

It didn't have to be that way.

It was such a simple thing in the end. To notice. To stay.

Yet, it had made all the difference.
