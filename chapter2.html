Chapter Two

No Second Chances

Ijbol67 67K views

I didn't really know who Mae was at our first meeting.

It was only two weeks later that I discovered who Mae truly was.

· · ·

It started with an announcement that the whole school was to gather in the AVR for a student council address. Nothing unusual about that. We lined up outside, section by section, and made our way to the AVR while the teachers tried to keep everyone from making too much noise.

I sat with Bea and Lara near the middle, not really paying attention to anything in particular. People around us were already talking to each other, filling the room with the usual noise that came with any school-wide assembly.

Then the student council officers stepped on the stage.

"Oh, that's the president," Bea said, nodding toward the front.

"Which one?" Lara asked.

"The first one." Bea paused. "Wait, no. I think that's the secretary."

"Then who's the president?"

At that moment, someone stepped up to the podium, and the noise around us shifted slightly. The kind of shift that happens when someone walks into a room and people start paying attention without quite knowing why.

I looked up.

It was Mae.

"Wait," I said. "I know that person."

Lara glanced at me. "You do?"

"That's Mae. I met them in the lobby a couple weeks ago."

"That's Mae?" Bea straightened up. "Christine, that's not just anyone. Mae is one of the highest-ranking officers in the student council. Either VP or Pres."

"And varsity," someone in front of us said. "Karate club I think."

"And always greets people in the hallway," someone behind us mentioned. "Even us lower batches."

I looked at the stage again. Mae stood at the podium with the kind of posture that looked completely natural but somewhat practiced. Composed. Collected. The type of person who looked like they belonged exactly where they were.

"Mae is literally so impressive," Bea said, a little dreamily. "I wish I had that kind of presence."

The program began, and Mae opened with a few welcoming remarks before moving on to the main announcement: the upcoming Halloween Music Showcase. Mae discussed the event details clearly and confidently.

Mae was good at this. That was obvious.

But somewhere in the middle of it, something happened.

It was a small thing. A line that came out in the wrong order. A brief pause where there shouldn't have been one. A glance down at the paper that lasted a second too long.

The audience didn't notice it. People were still half-distracted, whispering to their friends. The applause at the end was warm, the kind that meant everyone was satisfied.

But I had been watching.

And I saw the way Mae tightened for just a moment before smiling again.

I saw the way the paper was held a little too tightly on the way off the stage.

Small things. The kind most people wouldn't bother to notice.

But I noticed.

· · ·

I almost didn't say anything.

It wasn't really my place, after all. We spoke to each other just one time, for less than five minutes, over a school bell. Mae probably didn't even remember my name.

But after the assembly was dismissed and the crowd started returning back to their classrooms, I found myself going to the direction I last saw Mae walk off.

Mae was standing a few meters away from the other officers, looking down at the printed speech notes. Not reading them exactly. More like staring at them.

"Hi," I said while waving.

Mae looked up at me.

"Christine, right?"

Wow. Mae remembered me. I felt quite happy about that.

"Yep," I said. "Great job up there."

"Thank you."

Two words. Steady voice. But the paper in Mae's hand was folded and refolded at the corner. A small, restless crease formed at the edge.

"Are you okay?" I asked.

Mae blinked. "Of course. Why?"

"I don't know. You just looked a little..."

"I'm fine." The response came quickly. "Everything went well."

I hesitated. I wanted to say, “not everything, not exactly.” But I didn't. Because who was I to point that out? Mae stood in front of the entire school and held it together. Whatever slipped was small enough that no one else caught it.

Maybe I was just imagining things.

"Okay," I said. "That's good."

Mae smiled. The same one I saw on the stage, the same one I saw in the hallway. Easy. Practiced.

"I have to get back to the other officers," Mae said. "But it was nice seeing you again, Christine."

"You too."

And just like that, Mae stepped back into the group, back into the role, back into everything that everyone expected.

I stood there for a moment longer than I should have.

Mae left the paper with the speech notes on the table nearby. I almost didn't look at it.

But I did.

The lines that came out in the wrong order during the speech were circled in red ink, with a few handwritten words written in the margin beside them.

Unacceptable.

Do better next time.

No second chances.

I put down the paper and walked away.

But those few words stuck with me for the rest of the day.
