Prologue

We all know that one person from school.

The one who raises their hand at every question for recitation points. The one who seems to be active everywhere, from varsity to student council. The one who greets everybody in the hallway, even the other batches.

It seems as if life has never once been unfair to them.

They have all the skills needed for success. They seem to have their life all figured out. They can do whatever they want with their life.

The world is their oyster.

Everyone knows them.

Everyone loves them.

And honestly? I used to think I did too.

But here's the thing about perfect people.

They are masters of disguise and illusion. They are great at keeping some distance to make you feel as if you're watching them from the other side of a glass wall. Close enough to see, but never close enough to really know.

The more I observed, the more I noticed something.

Small things. The kind of things most people would never bother to notice.

But I noticed.

· · ·

Maybe I should have just stayed to myself. Maybe I should not have intervened.

Maybe it was best to let them keep being everyone's favorite person.

Maybe.

But my restlessness got the better of me.

There was something about them that told me that the person we saw in school wasn't the whole story.

And I want to find out who this person really is.
